<objective>
Strip the bidirectional merge from KeypointTracker and re-evaluate.

Background: Phase 84-01 revealed the bidi merge is both buggy and valueless:
- The merge uses 1:1 Hungarian assignment, but the forward pass fragments fish into
  multiple tracks while the backward pass keeps them as one. This N:1 mismatch causes
  unmatched forward fragments to survive as duplicate tracklets (same fish, two IDs,
  identical centroids). 8 duplicate pairs found, inflating track count from ~37 to 44.
- The single-pass tracker already has ORU (observation-centric re-update) and OCR
  (observation-centric recovery) — the same OC-SORT mechanisms that handle occlusion
  recovery. Forward-only produces 37 tracks with 0.942 coverage; backward-only produces
  36 tracks with 0.942 coverage. The bidi merge adds no value.
- Forward-only (37 tracks, 94.2% coverage) is strictly better than bidi (44 tracks,
  89.8% coverage) on every metric.

This plan strips the bidi machinery and re-runs the evaluation to get clean numbers.
</objective>
